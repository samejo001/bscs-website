About Program

What is BSCS?

A comprehensive four-year degree designed to build world-class computer scientists.

Degree Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) is a rigorous four-year program combining theoretical foundations with practical software engineering skills.

4 Years Duration
8 Semesters Structure
136 Credits Total

Mission

To produce graduates who can design, develop, and analyze complex computing systems while adapting to emerging technologies.

Vision

To be a leading center of excellence in computer science education, research, and innovation recognized globally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the BSCS program.

Applicants need a minimum 60% in FSc Pre-Engineering or equivalent with Mathematics. Entry tests may be required depending on the institution. Some universities also accept ICS and FSc Pre-Medical with additional math courses.

The BSCS is a 4-year degree program consisting of 8 semesters. It typically requires 130-140 credit hours including core courses, electives, and a final year project.

You will learn C++, Java, Python, JavaScript, SQL, and C# throughout the program. Additional languages like PHP, R, and Go may be covered in electives.

Yes, we have partnerships with 150+ tech companies. Students can apply for summer internships after their 4th semester and mandatory internships in the final year.

Graduates can pursue roles as Software Engineers, Web Developers, AI Engineers, Data Scientists, Cybersecurity Analysts, Cloud Engineers, UI/UX Designers, and more. Many also start their own tech companies.

Yes, students must complete a capstone project in their final year (2 semesters). Projects can be research-based, industry-sponsored, or entrepreneurial ventures with faculty mentorship.
